15.1 ORIGIN AND DURATION

Dialogue 1

How long have you been playing?

I've been playing since beginning of last term? I've been playing for about two years now.

Dialogue 2:

When did you learn how to play waltz?

I learnt a few weeks ago. I learnt in 1970.

Dialogue 3: 

How long have you known each other? We've known each other about six months now. We met about a year ago.

1.     

a.      We use the Present Perfect continuous with for and since to talk about how long a present situation has been going on, that is its duration.

b.      For refers to a period of time (e.g. for a month) and since refers to a point of time (e.g. since June).

2.     

a.      We use the past simple with ago and in to talk about when a regent situation started, that is its origin.

b.      Ago refers to a period of time (e.g. a month ago) and in refers to a point of time. (e.g. in July)

3.    There are certain verbs, e.g. be., own, know, believe and have (meaning 'possess'), which have no continuous form. With these verbs 'the present perfect simple is used instead of the Present Perfect Continuous. Therefore, in dialogue 3, the speaker says 'have known' and not 'have been knowing'.
