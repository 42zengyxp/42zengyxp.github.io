11.3 PREFERENCES

1.     

A: Which do you prefer: driving a car or being a passenger?

B: Well, I enjoy being a passenger if the road is good and the scenery is beautiful. I can sit and look around.


A: So, you don't like being a passenger if the road is bad?

B: That's right. I don't think other people can drive well on bad roads. On the whole, I prefer being a passenger in a good driver's car.

2.     

A: Which do you prefer: swimming in a pool or swimming in the sea?

B: Well, that depends on weather. If the sea is rough, I don't enjoy swimming there. The water there is dirty and I don't feel it easy.

A: So, you prefer a pool, then?

B: Only when the sea is rough, I feel a pool quite narrow. I can't go a long distance there I don't feel that I’m in water. On the whole, I prefer swimming in the sea to swimming in a pool.

3.     

A: Which do you prefer: sharing a flat or living alone?

B: Well, that depends on the room-mate. I like the person who doesn't mind my singing and dancing. I love spicy food. I'm not very fond of arguments.

A: So, you like a person who does not talk very much?

B: I don't like to talk unnecessarily. I enjoy dancing and singing alone I'm fond of shopping. On the whole, I prefer living alone to sharing a flat.

4.     

A: Which do you prefer: working outside or working inside?

B: Well, that depends on the nature of jobs. I love talking. I can't stand sitting alone and working with the machine. I'm crazy about visiting places but I hate walking.

A: So, you don't like to work in an office?

B: Yes, but I enjoy sitting by a reception desk. I love the work which has public relations. On the whole, I prefer a field work which does’ t involve walking.

11. 4 YOUR OWN LIKES AND DISLIKES

1.     

A: How do you feel about travelling?

B: I'm fond of travelling, but I hate travelling by bus. I adore riding a motorbike.

2.     

A: How do you feel about eating cut?

B: I can’t stand eating in a cheap restaurant, but I don't mind eating in a five-star hotel.

3.     

A: Do you enjoy parties?

B: I'm not very fond of wedding parties, but I like birthday parties.

4.     

A: What do you like to do in the summer?

B: I love swimming in a pool, but I loathe walking in the sun.

5.     

A: What types of clothes do you like?

B. I’m crazy about jeans, but I hate polyester.  

6.     

A: How do you feel when it’s raining?

B: I can't stand walking in the rain, but I love to see the rainwater flowing.  

7.     

 A: How do you feel when you’re in class?

 B. I enjoy the lecture when I understand it, but when the topic is vague, I don't like it.

8.     

A: How do you feel when you’re alone?

B: I like very much. I can pass the time the way I like. I love reading, but I can't stand loneliness.

11.6 TYPES OF PEOPLE

1.    A football star loves people asking him for his autograph, but he doesn't like being pushed about by crowds of his fans. He likes having his way of playing fairly described in newspapers. He doesn't mind being told what to do by his coach, but he hates being ordered about by the club president. He loves having his work properly praised and he adores being told what a wonderful player he is.

2.    A beggar loves people giving him a lot of money. He hates people asking him to work instead of begging. He doesn't mind being told what dirty clothes he has. He can't stand the children running after him. He doesn't mind being laughed at. He doesn't like the dogs barking at him. He adores people giving him warm clothes and good food.

3.    A journalist loves people informing him about an important event. He doesn't mind other criticizing his articles. He hates the police stopping him from going to the important place. He enjoys people letting him to take photographs. He hates people asking him unnecessary questions. He doesn't mind being told what to do by the editors.  

4.    A small child likes people admiring his clothes. He adores people swing him beautiful toys. He hates people waking him up early in the morning. He doesn't like people criticizing his behavior. He likes having his work praised. He doesn’t mind whether he's given money or not. He loves people giving him sweets and ice- creams. He enjoys people tickling him.
