9.3 WHICH WOULD YOU RATHER?

Writing 

I’d rather live in a village than in a city. A village is fresher than a city because air is not polluted there by industry and vehicles. It's lovelier because of forests, fields and rivers. It's more inspiring to the poets. It's quieter because there's no noise of the people and vehicles. It's more delightful and beautiful. It's closer to the nature. Villagers are simpler and more innocent. So, a village is more praised by poets and writers.

9.4 COMPARISON OF ADVERBS

Answers: Mostly one-syllabled adverbs with the same form as adjectives are made into comparative forms by using -er. e.g. harder, faster. Adverbs that are formed from 'adjective + ly' are used as comparative forms by using more, e.g. more beautifully, more neatly, etc.

1.    He gets up early, doesn't he?

A: He gets up early, doesn't he?

B: Oh, I don't know. I get up earlier than he does.

A: Of course- nobody gets up as early as you do.

2.    She smokes heavily, doesn't she?

A: She smokes heavily, doesn't she?

B: Oh, I don't know. I smoke much more heavily than she does.

A: Of course—nobody smokes as heavily as you do.

3.    He played well, didn't he?

A: He played well, didn't he?

B: Oh, I don't know. I played much better than he did.

A: Of course- nobody played as well as you did.

4.    He can dance beautifully, can't he?

A: He can dance beautifully, can't he?

B: Oh, I don't know. I can dance much more beautifully than he can.

A: Nobody can dance as beautifully as you can.

5.    They live dangerously, don't they?

A: They live dangerously, don't they?

 B: Oh, I don't know. I live much more dangerously than they do.

A: Of course—nobody lives as dangerously as you do.

6.    She can run fast, can't she?

A: She can run fast, can't she?

B: Oh, I don't know, I can run faster than she can.

A: Of course-nobody can run as fast as you can.

7.     They stay up late, don’t they?

A: They stay up late, don't they?

B: Oh, I don't know. I stay much later than they do.

A:  Of course- nobody stays as late as you do.

8.    She behaved badly, didn't she?

A: She behaved badly, didn't she?

B: Oh, I don't know. I behaved much worse than she did.

A: Of course-nobody behaved as badly as you did.

9.6 SALARY SCALES 

Nurses are trained for two years after they complete their SEE. During their training they have to study and go to visit hospitals. In the end their qualification is equivalent to Intermediate Level. They have to work eight hours a day. Sometimes they have to work in the night and in the day. They work in neat and clean hospitals. There is no risk involved in their job. Normally, they are satisfied with their job. Their responsibility is high. On duty they have to take care of their patients very well. 


Policemen are trained for one year. Their training is very hard. In the end their educational qualification does not improve. They are supposed to be employed twenty-four hours a day. Their job is quite risky. They might be killed on duty. They are satisfied with their jobs if there is disturbance in the country. Their responsibility is more. They have to watch the arrested persons carefully.


Miners are trained for a few weeks. No educational qualification is needed for them. Their training does not promote any educational qualification. They have to work eight hours a day. They have a to work in a very unhealthy environment. To work underground is quite risky. They might be killed. Most of the miners aren't satisfied with their jobs. They are responsible to their employers. 


High school teachers are trained for one year if they are graduate. They have to study and visit schools for practice teaching. In the end of their training, they become technical graduate. Normally they take twenty-four classes a week. But, they have to keep themselves busy preparing their lesson plans and correcting their students’ answer books. They work in the classrooms or in the teachers' room. Most of the teachers are satisfied with their jobs. Their responsibility is more. They are the makers of the future society.


Long distance lorry drivers are trained for six months. They learn how to drive. They have to be familiar with highway signposts. Their training does not help them to promote their educational qualification. Their working hours are not fixed. Sometimes they have to drive more than 15 hours a day. Driving all the time is a boring job. But visiting new places and new people makes them happy. They are responsible to their employers. They have to avoid knocking dawn people and animals along the road.
