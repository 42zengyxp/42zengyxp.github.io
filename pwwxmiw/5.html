5.2 BEFORE, AFTER AND WHILE 

1.    The pictures show seven periods in Geoffrey's life. Look at the arrows above the pictures. What events does each point to?

The first arrow points to the events that he left or finished school and went to college. The second arrow points to the events that he left or graduated from college and joined the army or became an army captain. The third arrow points to the event that he was promoted to general. The fourth arrow points to the events that he left or retired from the army and went into or got involved in politics. The fifth arrow points to the event that he was arrested or-was sent to prison. The sixth arrow points to the events that he came out of prison or was released and retired or went to the Bahamas.


2.    The arrows below the pictures show other events in his life. In groups, ask and answer questions about when each happened.

Example:

A: When did he meet Angeline?

B: He met her before he became a captain.

C: He met her after he went to college.

D: He met her while he was at college.

Answers:

1.     

A: When did he marry Angeline?

B: He married her before he became a general.

C: He married her after he graduated from college.

D: He married her while he was a captain.

2.     

A: When did he sell his military secrets?

B: He sold them before he went into politics.

C: He sold them after he was promoted to a general.

D: He sold them while he was a general.

3.     

A: When did Angeline leave him?

B: She left him before he was arrested.

C: She left him after he went into politics. She left him while he was in politics.

A: When did he write a book about his life? 

B: He wrote it before he went to live in the Bahamas.

C: He wrote it after he was sent to prison.

D: He wrote it while he was in prison.

5.4 LIFE STORY

Writing

David Manning was born in London in 1942, and attended Westminster School for Boys. He left school in 1959. A year later he wrote his first novel Rag Doll. A year after that he went to Indonesia. While he was there, he met his wife in 1965. Four years after that he left Indonesia, and bought of a farm in the West of England. But three years later he gave it up and started making documentaries. Two years later, his best-seller, The Cold Earth, came out, and he gave up TV work. While he was in Indonesia, he was inspired to make the film Eastern Moon, but he didn't make it until 1978.

Past Times

a.    at- 4 0' clock, the end of May, Christmas, the turn of the century

c.    in - May, the summer, 1945, the 1920s, Victorian times, the 19th century, the Middle Ages
