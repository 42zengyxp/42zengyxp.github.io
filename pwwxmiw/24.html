24. 6 Not What You’d Expect

Change the sentences below in the same way, (a) with although/even though (b) with in spite of/ despite.

1.    She was 85 years old, but she still lived a very active life.

2.    Video machines are expensive, but lots of people are buying them.

3.    Her parents objected, but she still insisted on getting married.

4.    Beethoven was deaf, but he continued composing until his death.

5.    The acoustics in the hall are poor, but it is still regularly used for concerts.

6.    We've known each other for a long time, but we still call each other by our surnames.

7.    Hammerfest is a long way north of the Arctic Circle, but the sea never freezes there.

Answers:

1.    Even though she was 85 years old, she still lived a very active life.

 In spite of her old age, she still lived a very active life.

2.    Although video machines are expensive, lots of people are buying them.

In spite of the cost (expense), lots of people are buying video machines.

3.    Even though her parents objected, she still insisted on getting married.

Despite her parents' objections, she still insisted on getting married.

4.    Although Beethoven was deaf, he continued composing until his death.

Despite his deafness, Beethoven continued composing until his death.

5.    Even though the acoustics in the hall are poor, it is still regularly used for concerts.

In spite of its poor acoustics, the hall is still regularly used for concerts.

6.    Although we've known each other for a long time, we still call each other by our surnames.

In spite of the length of time we've known each other, we still call each other by our surnames.

7.    Even though Hammerfest is a long way north of the Arctic Circle, the sea never freezes there.

Despite the distance of Hammerfest, north of the Arctic Circle, the sea never freezes there.
