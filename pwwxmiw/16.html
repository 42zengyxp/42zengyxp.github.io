Solution of Meaning into Words of Class XI

Unit 16

16.1 IN, ON AND AT

We use in to show that something is completely inside something and to show that something is in an area with boundaries e.g. chairs in the dining room and cows in the field. We use on to show that something is on a line or on a surface, e.g. a table on the bottle and a town on the lake. We use at to show that something is at or near a point, e.g. at a bus stop.

Practice

1.    In a river we might find water, people swimming, islands, fish, etc. On water we might find boats, ice, towns, tree. etc.

2.    In a bottle we might find water, wine, a cork, etc. On a bottle we might a label, dust, etc.

3.     In a bed we might find someone asleep, insects, etc. On a bed we might find blankets, someone having a rest, etc.

4.    On a door we might find a knocker, a message, a number, etc. At a door we might find a visitor, a beggar, etc.  

5.    In the grass we might find insects, a snake, etc. (long grass). On the grass we might find people sitting, etc. (short grass).

6.    In a swimming pool we might find people swimming, etc. At a swimming pool we might find people changing, watching, drinking tea, etc.

7.    In a corner (indoors) we might find a lamp, a wastepaper basket, etc. At a corner (of a street) we might find someone waiting, a letter box, etc.
