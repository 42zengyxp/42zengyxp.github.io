20.2 THE LOST PROPERTY OFFICE

Assistant: Good afternoon, sir.

Man: Good afternoon. I wonder if you can help. I’ve lost my bag.

Assistant: Where did you lose it, sir?

Man: I left it on the trolley bus yesterday morning.

Assistant: Can you describe it?

Man: Well, it’s a small handbag with a stripe pattern. It has a white strap to hold on.

Assistant: Hmm. I’m afraid we haven’t gone anything like that, sir. Sorry.

Man: Well, to tell you the truth, I lost another bag on the bus last week. It's a shoulder bag with zips and a pocket. It has a long strap to hang it on the shoulder. It has a 'PANAM' pattern on the side.

Assistant: Sorry, sir. Nothing like that.

Man: Hmm. And then only this morning I left my suitcase in a park. It has a small handle, a check pattern and a tag hanging on the side.

Assistant: Look, sir, I'm a busy man. If you really need a bag so badly, there's a very good second-hand bag shop just round the corner.

Composition

1.    Electronic Games

An electronic game is a game controlled by very small computer called a

microprocessor. All electronic games have 'a program (set of instructions for the computer). Most electronic games are connected to a viewing screen called a screen. For this reason, the games are often called video games.


Most video games are colorful. They have rapidly changing visual effects and complicated sound effects. Many are modelled or war themes or space travel. There are also electronic versions of sport card games, board games, etc.


Players operate the games by pushing buttons and levers called joysticks. People play against each other or against the computer itself. The first video game, Pong, appeared in the early 1970's. In 1979, the electronic game industry developed rapidly with the popularity of a game called Space Invaders. Nintendo became the most popular video game of the 1980's.

2.    Video Machines

By 'video machines' we understand video cameras video discs and video cassette recorders. But in this article, we shall deal with a video cassette recorder. A video cassette recorder (VCR) is a device that records visual images and sound on magnetic tape. It also plays back the recorded video (picture) and audio (sound) information on television sets.


Video cassette recorders were first used by the television broadcasting industry during the 1950s. They have become a part of home entertainment systems since 1970s. VCRs are designed to enable consumers to tape television programs while they are away from home and watch the program at their convenience.

3.    Nuclear Weapons

Nuclear weapon is any weapon that gets its destructive power from the transformation of matter in atoms into energy. All nuclear weapons are explosive devices. They include missiles bombs, artillery shells, mines and torpedoes. The most powerful ones are far more destructive than any nonmolecular ones.

4.    Plastic Surgery

Plastic surgery is a field of medicine that specializes in the repair or reshaping of defects of the body. It also may involve the repair of muscles, bones, blood vessels, and nerves. Plastic surgeons shape and mould body tissues. They rearrange, remove, or replace aged body parts. Plastic surgeons also attempt to improve the appearance of ageing tissue.


     Methods of plastic surgery include grafting (transferring tissue), repairing or reshaping tissue structures, removing tissue and replacing tissue with artificial substance. Grafting is used in treating such cases as severe burns, gunshot wounds and injuries resulting from traffic accidents. Birth defects of the face, ears, hands, and genitals can be corrected by rebuilding and reshaping tissues. Similarly, a deformed nose can be reshaped.
